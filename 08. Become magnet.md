# Law 8: Make Other People Come to You

Forget the title.

The core idea:

> **The person who chases usually gives away power. The person who is chased has options.**

---

## The Fishing Story 🎣

A fisherman has two choices.

### Option 1:

Jump into the water and chase fish.

The fish run away.

### Option 2:

Stay still, put bait, and let fish come.

The fisherman controls the situation.

That's Law 8.

---

## Human Example

Imagine two people looking for a job.

Person A:

> "Please hire me. I need this job."

They have less power.

Person B:

Builds:

* skills
* reputation
* results

Companies approach them.

They can choose.

Who has more control?

Person B.

---

## The Hidden Psychology

When you chase something, you communicate:

> "I need you."

The other side feels valuable.

When people come to you, they communicate:

> "You have something I need."

The balance changes.

---

## Relationship Example

Someone ignores you.

You keep texting:

"Why aren't you replying?"

"Please talk."

You are chasing.

Now imagine you focus on your own life.

Your attention becomes valuable again.

The dynamic changes.

---

## Business Example

A weak business says:

> "Buy my product, please."

A strong business creates something people naturally want.

Customers come.

---

## Memory Hook 🧲

Don't be a person running after magnets.

Become a magnet.

Build something that attracts.

---

## Important Note

This does NOT mean:

"Never approach anyone."

You still need action.

The lesson is:

> **Don't put yourself in a position where you are begging for value.**

Create value first.

---

## One Sentence To Remember:

> **"Don't chase the fish; become the bait."**

Your chain:

1. Make people feel important
2. Find the invisible rope
3. Protect the map
4. Control words
5. Protect reputation
6. Get noticed
7. Multiply force
8. Become the attraction

That's Law 8.
