# Law 6: Court Attention at All Costs

Forget the title.

The core idea:

> **If people don't notice you, your value may never get a chance to work.**

A hidden treasure and a visible treasure are treated differently.

---

## The Dark Room Example 💎

Imagine a diamond lying in a dark room.

It is still a diamond.

But nobody knows.

Now put it under bright light.

People notice.

The diamond didn't change.

Only visibility changed.

That is attention.

---

## The Brutal Truth

The world does not only reward:

* talent
* intelligence
* hard work

It also rewards:

* visibility
* reputation
* presence

A person with average skill and huge attention can sometimes get opportunities that a brilliant but invisible person never receives.

---

## Example

Two people create the same product.

### Person A

* builds quietly
* tells nobody
* waits

### Person B

* shows progress
* explains the idea
* builds a reputation

Even before success comes, people know Person B.

When opportunities appear, who comes to mind first?

Usually the visible one.

---

## But Attention Is Dangerous

Not all attention is good.

A person can become famous for:

* talent
* stupidity
* controversy

Attention is like fire.

It can:

* cook your food
* burn your house

You need control.

---

## The Psychology

Humans notice what stands out.

A crowded street has 100 shops.

Which one do you remember?

The one with:

* the unique sign
* the loud design
* the different idea

The invisible gets ignored.

---

## The Actor Example 🎭

A great actor performing in an empty theatre:

Amazing performance.

No audience.

No influence.

A less talented actor on a huge stage:

Everyone knows him.

Attention multiplies whatever is already there.

---

## Memory Hook 👀

Imagine you have a flashlight.

Your talent is the object.

Attention is the light.

Without light, people cannot see the object.

---

## One Sentence To Remember

> **"Power needs an audience; invisible strength is often treated as no strength."**

The chain so far:

1. Respect people's ego.
2. Find the hidden motivation.
3. Protect your plans.
4. Control what you reveal.
5. Guard your reputation.
6. Make sure you are seen.

That's Law 6.

Notice how many successful people are not just good — they are noticeably good.
