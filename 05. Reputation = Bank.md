# Law 5: So Much Depends on Reputation

Forget the title.

The core idea:

> **Before people know you, they know your reputation.**

Your reputation is like a **shadow**.

It enters the room before you do.

---

## The Story: Two Doctors 🩺

Doctor A:

Everyone says:
> "He is extremely careful. He never makes mistakes."

Doctor B:

People say:
> "He is careless."

Both doctors make the same small mistake.

Who gets forgiveness?

Usually Doctor A.

Why?

The mistake is judged through the lens of reputation.

---

## The Hidden Game

People don't have time to study everyone deeply.

So the brain uses shortcuts.

It asks:

> "What kind of person is this?"

That answer becomes your reputation.

---

## The Currency Example 💰

Imagine your reputation is a bank account.

Every action is a deposit or withdrawal.

Deposit:
- keeping promises
- being consistent
- doing good work

Withdrawal:
- lying
- breaking trust
- being unreliable

A person with a full account gets chances.

A person with an empty account gets suspicion.

---

## The Brutal Part

People often don't judge your intention.

They judge your pattern.

You say:

> "I didn't mean to be late."

They think:

> "He is always late."

Your reputation is your history repeating in people's minds.

---

## The Chess Example ♟️

A chess player is known for attacking aggressively.

Before the match begins, the opponent is already thinking:

> "I must defend."

The reputation itself creates an advantage.

---

## The Trap

A good reputation gives power.

But a damaged reputation can trap you.

Once people label you:

- "lazy"
- "dishonest"
- "weak"

They start interpreting everything you do through that label.

---

## Memory Hook 🪪

Imagine every person carries an invisible ID card.

Not your name.

Your reputation.

People read that card before they talk to you.

---

## One Sentence To Remember

> **"Your reputation is the person people meet before they meet you."**

The first 4 laws:

1. Make people feel important.  
2. Find the invisible rope.  
3. Hide your map.  
4. Control your words.  
5. Protect your shadow.

That's Law 5. Keep watching for it — you will see it everywhere.
