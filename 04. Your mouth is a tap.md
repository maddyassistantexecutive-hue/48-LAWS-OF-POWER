# Law 4: Always Say Less Than Necessary

Forget the title.

The real idea:

> **The more you reveal, the more power you give away.**

Words are information.

Information is leverage.

---

## The Cup Story ☕

Imagine two cups.

### Cup A: Half full

People wonder:

> "What's inside?"

Curiosity pulls them.

### Cup B: You pour everything out

Everyone knows.

No mystery.

No curiosity.

People move on.

Humans are naturally attracted to what they don't fully understand.

---

## The Common Mistake

Someone asks:

> "Why did you do that?"

Many people answer:

> "I did it because yesterday I was thinking about this, then this happened, and my friend said this, and I felt..."

They just gave away:

* their emotions
* their fears
* their reasoning
* their weaknesses

The other person now has a map of your mind.

---

## The Power Example

In a negotiation:

Person A:

> "I need this deal because my deadline is tomorrow."

They just revealed weakness.

Person B now knows:

> "Time pressure."

The leverage changed.

---

## Silence Does Something Interesting

When you stop talking, other people often start talking.

Why?

The human brain dislikes empty space.

Silence creates pressure.

People fill it.

And when they fill it, they reveal information.

---

## The Restaurant Example 🍛

You ask a waiter:

> "Which dish is best?"

A weak answer:

> "Everything is good, sir. The biryani is nice, the dosa is nice, actually yesterday a customer said..."

Too much.

A strong answer:

> "The biryani."

Simple.

Confidence.

---

## The Dark Side

Talking too much often comes from wanting:

* approval
* validation
* attention

The person who says less often appears:

* calm
* confident
* controlled

---

## Memory Hook 🪣

Imagine your mouth is a water tap.

Every unnecessary word opens the tap.

Powerful people don't leave the tap running.

They control the flow.

---

## One Sentence To Remember

> **"Every word you release gives away a piece of your position."**

Law 1:

> Make people feel important.

Law 2:

> Find the rope.

Law 3:

> Hide the map.

Law 4:

> Control the information.

Don't move on until you start noticing how often people talk themselves into weakness.
