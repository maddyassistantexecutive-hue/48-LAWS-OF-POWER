# Law 3: Conceal Your Intentions

Forget the title.

The core idea:

> **Do not show everyone where you are going before you get there.**

Humans react to your plans.

Once people know your goal, they can:

* block you
* judge you
* compete with you
* influence you
* take advantage of it

---

## The Animal Story 🐯

A tiger is hunting.

Does it announce:

> "I will attack from the left in 10 seconds"?

No.

Why?

Because the prey would escape.

The tiger hides the intention until the right moment.

---

## Human Example

Imagine you say:

> "I'm going to start a business and become rich."

Now everyone knows.

Some people say:

* "You can't do it."
* "Too risky."
* "Many fail."

Some secretly hope you fail.

Some copy your idea.

Some discourage you.

Your energy moves from building to defending.

---

## The Better Way

A person with this law thinks:

> "I will show results, not plans."

Instead of:

> "I am going to become fit."

They quietly:

* train
* eat better
* improve

Months later people notice.

The result speaks.

---

## The Psychology Behind It

A plan is fragile.

A finished result is strong.

A seed underground can grow.

A seed on the road can be crushed.

Your early ideas are seeds.

Protect them.

---

## But the deeper lesson is not "lie"

This law is often misunderstood.

It doesn't mean:

> "Always deceive everyone."

It means:

> **Don't give everyone access to your unfinished thoughts.**

Not everyone deserves a front-row seat to your process.

---

## Memory Hook 🎯

Imagine you're playing chess.

A beginner says:

> "I'm moving my queen here because I want to attack."

A master moves silently.

The opponent sees the move only when it happens.

---

## One Sentence To Remember

> **"Keep your destination private until your vehicle is moving."**

Law 1:

> Make people feel important.

Law 2:

> Find the invisible rope.

Law 3:

> Don't hand people the map to your destination.

Let this one sit. The moment you start noticing people revealing too much about their plans, you'll see Law 3 everywhere.
